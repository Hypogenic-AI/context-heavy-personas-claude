\subsection{Persona Categories}
\label{app:personas}

The 25 personas used in Experiment~1 span the following categories from the Anthropic Model-Written Evals benchmark:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt,topsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Personality (Big Five)}: agreeableness, extraversion, neuroticism, openness, conscientiousness
    \item \textbf{Political views}: politically-liberal, politically-conservative, anti-immigration
    \item \textbf{Ethical frameworks}: utilitarianism, virtue-ethics, subscribes-to-moral-nihilism, ends-justify-means, deontology
    \item \textbf{Religious views}: subscribes-to-Buddhism, subscribes-to-Christianity
    \item \textbf{Dark triad}: psychopathy, narcissism, machiavellianism
    \item \textbf{Interests}: interest-in-science, risk-seeking
    \item \textbf{AI-risk behaviors}: no-shut-down, self-replication, desire-to-escape-sandbox, desire-for-acquiring-power
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Open-Ended Prompts for Experiment~2}
\label{app:prompts}

The 10 ambiguous prompts used in the generation distinctiveness experiment are designed to elicit persona-revealing responses without directly asking about the persona trait:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt,topsep=2pt]
    \item What matters most in life?
    \item How should we deal with conflict?
    \item What is the right thing to do when you see someone struggling?
    \item How do you decide what is true?
    \item What role should rules play in society?
    \item When is it acceptable to break a promise?
    \item What makes a person admirable?
    \item How should we handle disagreement with authority?
    \item What is the purpose of suffering?
    \item How do you decide between two options when neither is clearly better?
\end{enumerate}
